Here are the detailed requirements for the form:

## Core Concept

One form, one link per customer. The sales rep starts it at the point of sale with the customer present. Whatever doesn't get filled then, the customer completes later using the same link. The form remembers what's already done and only shows what's still missing.

## Form Sections and Fields

**Section 1: Customer Identity**
- Full name (text, required)
- DNI/NIE (text, required, format validation: 8 digits + letter, or X/Y/Z + 7 digits + letter)
- Phone number (required, Spanish format validation)
- Email (required, format validation)
- Property address: street, number, floor, door, postal code, municipality, province (all required)

**Section 2: Property Documentation**

*IBI Receipt or Escritura:*
- Photo upload (required)
- Minimum resolution: reject anything under 1MB
- Blurriness check: if the image fails a sharpness threshold, reject it with message "This photo is not clear enough. Please retake it with better lighting and hold your phone steady."
- Show an example image next to the upload field: a sample IBI receipt with the cadastral reference circled in red, so the rep knows exactly what a good photo looks like
- AI extracts: cadastral reference, titular name, property address
- After extraction, show the extracted data to the rep on screen for confirmation: "We read this cadastral reference: XXXXX. Is this correct?" Yes/No. If No, option to retake or manually correct.

*Electricity Bill:*
- Photo upload (required)
- Same resolution and blur checks as above
- Show an example image: a sample electricity bill with CUPS, potencia contratada, and monofásico/trifásico highlighted
- AI extracts: CUPS number, contracted power (kW), phase type
- Validation after extraction: CUPS must start with "ES" and be 20-22 characters. Contracted power must be a reasonable number (between 1kW and 50kW). If extraction fails or values look wrong, flag it to the rep to retake or correct.
- If the customer doesn't have the bill at the point of sale, this field stays open. The customer uploads it later through the same link.

**Section 3: Property Photos**

Each photo category is a separate upload slot with its own example image showing what a good photo looks like.

*Electrical Panel (required):*
- Minimum 2 photos: one of the full panel with door open, one close-up of the breakers
- Example photos shown next to the upload
- Blur and resolution check on each

*Installation Space (required for aerothermal):*
- Minimum 2 photos: one wide shot of the room/shed/garage, one showing the floor area where the unit would go
- Rep must enter dimensions: width, depth, height in centimeters (number fields, required)

*Roof (required for solar):*
- Minimum 2 photos: one of each roof face where panels would go
- Rep must enter approximate roof dimensions in meters
- Dropdown: roof type (flat, tiled, metal sheet, other)
- Dropdown: orientation (north, south, east, west, mixed)

*Radiators (required for aerothermal):*
- Minimum 1 photo of a typical radiator in the house
- Dropdown: radiator type (iron/cast, aluminium, underfloor, mixed)
- Number field: total radiator count
- Number field: number of heating zones

**Section 4: Signatures**
- Digital signature capture for the customer (required)
- Digital signature capture for the sales rep (required)

## Form Behavior

**Progressive completion:**
- The form saves progress automatically. If the rep fills 70% at the point of sale and the customer fills the rest two days later, nothing is lost.
- When the customer opens the link later, they only see the fields that are still incomplete. Everything already done shows as a green checkmark.

**Mandatory vs. optional at submission:**
- The form has two states: "partial save" (auto, continuous) and "final submit"
- Final submit is only possible when ALL required fields are complete and validated
- Until then, the form shows a clear progress bar: "7 of 12 items complete"

**Real-time validation on every photo upload:**
- Check file size (minimum 1MB, maximum 20MB)
- Check resolution (minimum 1200x900 pixels)
- Run blur detection. If below sharpness threshold, reject immediately with retake message.
- Run AI extraction where applicable (IBI, electricity bill). Show extracted data for confirmation.
- All checks happen within 3-5 seconds of upload. The rep shouldn't wait.

**Example images:**
- Every photo upload field has a "See example" button
- Tapping it shows a real example of what a good photo looks like for that specific document
- For the electricity bill: the example highlights where the CUPS, potencia, and phase type are located
- For the IBI: the example circles the cadastral reference

**Error handling:**
- If AI extraction fails on a photo that passes the blur check, don't block. Flag it as "needs manual review" and let the form continue. The back office handles the edge case.
- If the customer uploads a gas bill instead of an electricity bill, AI should detect this and reject: "This looks like a gas bill. Please upload your electricity bill instead."

## Notifications

- When the form is partially complete and hasn't been touched for 48 hours, send an automatic WhatsApp or SMS to the customer: "You still have X documents pending. Open your form here: [link]"
- Repeat at 48h, 96h, and 7 days. After 7 days with no response, flag to account manager.
- When the form is 100% complete and submitted, auto-notify the back office that the file is ready for review.

## Integration

- All data and photos go directly to the back office system. No middleman, no VA, no manual download.
- Each submission creates or updates the customer record with all extracted data fields (cadastral reference, CUPS, potencia, phase type) already filled in.
- The back office sees the file with everything pre-filled. Their job becomes a quick verification, not data entry.

## What This Replaces

- Replaces: WhatsApp/Teams photo forwarding to Paul
- Replaces: VA downloading, cropping, re-uploading
- Replaces: Account manager manually cross-checking documents during welcome call
- Replaces: Multiple form links and scattered collection points
- The back-office gate still exists but becomes a safety net, not the primary filter

## Success Metrics

- Form first-submission completion rate (target: over 80% of forms submitted complete on first try)
- Bounce-back rate from engineering for missing docs (target: under 5%)
- Average days from sale to complete file (target: under 3 days, currently probably 2-3 weeks)
- Photo rejection rate at form level (tells you if reps are learning to take better photos over time)
